# Your site today vs. the new one

A plain-English comparison of what customers see at **thecivil.ca** right now versus what they'll see on the new site.

---

| What customers can do | thecivil.ca today | New site |
| --- | --- | --- |
| See the menu | Not on the site | Full menu, every item priced |
| Filter pies by veggie / meaty / spicy / sweet / dine-in only | No | Yes, one tap |
| See whether you're open right now | Hours table only | Live "Open now / Closed" badge in the header |
| Get directions | No map link | One tap to Google Maps (footer + visit page) |

## What the new site adds

### Your menu, finally

The current site doesn't show a menu at all. Most people decide where to eat by looking at one — without it, they default to the place that has one published. The new site lists **every item across six sections:**

- 15 pizzas (all $20)
- 3 starters
- 9 cocktails ($12, 1.5oz)
- 5 flights ($14, four 0.5oz pours)
- 5 beer & wine entries
- 5 lunch items (Wed–Sat, 12pm–4pm)

Every item has a price, a description, and a one-line tagline. Pies have a row of filter chips at the top — **Veggie, Meaty, Spicy, Sweet, Dine-in only** — so a vegetarian or someone looking for something specific can narrow down without scanning the whole list.

### A page for The Cause

Your monthly $3-per-pie charity rotation doesn't appear anywhere on the current site. The new site gives it:

- A dedicated page (`/the-cause`) with a 3-step explainer
- A continuously scrolling ribbon of past pizzas as a visual reminder
- A "nominate a charity" mailto link
- A bright red strip on the homepage so visitors see it before they leave

This is part of what makes The Civil The Civil. It belongs visible.

### A homepage that feels like the brand

The current homepage is a tagline ("Modern Cocktails. Adventurous Pies. Rotating Flights.") and a list of hours. The new site has:

- A big editorial hero — newspaper masthead lockup, a short subtitle, two CTAs
- A floating, animated **vapour bubble** that sits next to the hero. Tap it. It pops. It floats back up. It's a hint at the Bubble Infusion cocktail you actually serve, not a generic "wow" widget.
- A scrolling marquee of all 15 pizza names — clickable, each one jumps to that item on the menu page
- A 3-pizza featured grid showing your best photos
- The Cause strip
- A "The room" gallery of three interior shots
- An address-and-hours block at the bottom with **directions** and **call** buttons

A first-time visitor knows in 30 seconds whether you're their kind of spot.

### A cocktails page that shows the bar program

The current site implies cocktails exist. The new `/cocktails` page features a real photo of a negroni with the bubble overlaid, the full cocktail list, the flight list (with flavors), and a pinned panel explaining how flights work.

### A photo gallery that actually sells the food

**76 photos in total**, organized into:

- **The room** — interior shots, the bar, the pizza oven, the wallpaper, the neon
- **The pies** — 18 pizzas
- **Cocktails** — 12 drinks, including the pro-shoot negroni and pineapple-rosemary cocktail
- **Flights** — 9 flights including the gin & tonic, bourbon hot chocolate, and pastel
- **The block** — 4 exterior shots showing the storefront, the patio, the chalkboard

Plus a featured pair at the top for the strongest interior + exterior shot.

### Phone, address, and directions are one tap on every page

Today, a customer who finds your site and wants to call has to copy the address into Maps themselves. There's no phone number listed at all. The new site shows the address, phone, and a one-tap **Get directions** button on every page (in the footer and on `/visit`), plus the live phone number in the sticky action bar on phones.

### A discreet tap-to-call button on phones

On a mobile phone, a small phone-icon button sits in the bottom-right corner of every page. It doesn't get in the way, doesn't auto-hide on scroll, doesn't pulse — it's just there when someone wants to call. The earlier version of the site had a four-button bar (Reserve / Order / Directions / Call) but it ate too much screen and pulled focus from the actual content.

### A live "Open now / Closed" badge

Top-right of every page: a small badge that says whether you're currently open, what time you close (if you're open), or when you open next (if you're closed). Driven by your hours data, time-zoned to Toronto, refreshes every 60 seconds. No more "do they take walk-ins right now?" guessing.

### You edit the site yourself — `/admin`

The current site is the kind that needs a phone call (or a developer) to change. The new site has a **private admin area** at `/admin`:

- Sign in with your own username + password (`admin` / `thecivil`)
- **Contact & links** — phone, email, Instagram, Reserve URL, Order URL, Directions URL, address, tagline, search description
- **Hours** — every day's open/close ranges (multiple ranges per day for split shifts), the lunch window, which days lunch is served
- **Menu** — every section. Add a new pizza, change a price, change a tag, reorder, remove
- **Gallery** — drag in new photos, label them, mark category, set "feature" to put a photo at the top of the gallery, remove old photos
- One **Save and update site** button — your changes go live in about a minute

No developer, no waiting. Menu changes don't require anyone but you.

### Designed for The Civil specifically

Custom logo (hand-traced from your existing menu graphic), custom typography (Fraunces italic for the wordmark, JetBrains Mono for the small caps strip), a deliberate "newspaper × cocktail bar" look. Cream paper, ink black, ember red, brass gold. Not a builder template a thousand other restaurants are also using.

### One small joke

Try going to a page that doesn't exist. There's a hidden 16th pizza waiting there ("The Quiet Riot") that only customers who hit a 404 will ever see. Tiny detail, but it's the kind of thing that gets screenshotted.

### Search-friendly out of the box

Every page has a proper title, description, and structured data (`Restaurant` and `Menu` schemas) that Google understands. The current thecivil.ca shows a copyright placeholder of "2026" — small thing, but it tells search engines and customers that nobody's minding the store.
